Arctellis

The visual and verbal system behind the brand. Teal grounds every surface; azure carries the single live signal; the north-pointing chevron and monospace figures do the rest. Use these foundations exactly, and the brand reads as one firm across every touchpoint.

Teal-led palette Hanken Grotesk + JetBrains Mono UK English, no em-dashes

“We engineer operational advantage.” The system exists to make that promise feel true before a single word is read.

01 — Colour

Teal grounds. Azure signals. Nothing else competes.

Deep teal is the grounding ink and the default for CTAs, stat bands and the logo base. Azure is reserved as the one live accent: the engine line, active states, checks, a single beacon. Never let azure become decoration. Click a chip to copy its hex.

✓Do

Reserve azure for one live element per view: the analysing pulse, an active check, the forecast apex. Everything structural stays teal, ink and hairline grey.

✕Don't

Don't fill blocks, backgrounds or multiple buttons with azure, and never introduce a second bright hue. When azure is everywhere, it signals nothing.

02 — Typography

A grotesque for voice, a monospace for evidence.

Hanken Grotesk carries all display and body copy: tight at 800 for headlines, comfortable at 400 for reading. JetBrains Mono handles every figure, label and eyebrow, so numbers always look computed rather than claimed.

03 — Logo

A north-pointing chevron and one confident wordmark.

04 — Iconography & the chevron motif

One shape, echoed everywhere.

The chevron is the brand's connective tissue. It appears as a beacon, as the apex of a forecast line, as a bullet, and as a favicon. Supporting icons stay hairline and functional; numbers alongside them are always monospace.

05 — Voice & Tone

Plain, precise, and accountable for the outcome.

We write like a principal, not a vendor. Every claim is specific and quantified; every sentence earns its place. UK English throughout, and no em-dashes: use a comma, or a full stop.

01
Specific over grand

Name the decision, put a number on it. “£500–600k in the ordering decision” beats “significant value”.

02
Built, not advised

Language of engineering and ownership. We stay accountable for the outcome, not the recommendation.

03
Restraint is authority

Short sentences. No hype words, no exclamation, no jargon a client would not use themselves.

✓Write like this

“If it isn't embedded in the workflow, it's just a dashboard.”

“We find the decisions that carry the most value, put a number on them, and build the systems that change the numbers.”

“Proven in a controlled 90-day pilot.”

✕Not like this

“Leverage cutting-edge AI to unlock transformative synergies!”

“We deliver significant value across the enterprise” — vague, unquantified, could be anyone.

Em-dashes for asides — like this — belong to another brand.
